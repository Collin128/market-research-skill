# Questioning Chain: Four-Stage Strategic Analysis

Execute these four stages sequentially. Each stage builds on prior stages — explicitly reference earlier outputs when constructing later ones. Replace `{{industry}}` and `{{competitors}}` with actual values.

---

## Stage 1: The Unspoken Insight

**Prompt:**

You have deep research data on the {{industry}} market, covering competitors ({{competitors}}), customer sentiment, case studies, market conversation, expert perspectives, and industry news.

Answer this question in 1-2 paragraphs:

**What does every successful player in this market understand that their customers never say out loud?**

Do not summarize the research. Do not list obvious trends. Identify the unstated assumption or insight that separates the winners from the losers in this market. This should be something a 10-year industry veteran would nod at and say "that's exactly right" — not something you'd find in a blog post.

Ground your answer in specific evidence from the research data. If you can't identify something genuinely specific to this market, say so — a honest "I don't have enough signal" is better than a generic insight that could apply to any industry.

**Quality check:** Re-read your answer. If you could swap in a different industry name and the insight would still make sense, it's too generic. Rewrite it.

---

## Stage 2: Foundational Assumptions

**Prompt:**

Based on the research data and the Unspoken Insight you identified in Stage 1, examine the {{industry}} market and identify **3 structural assumptions** this market is built on.

For each assumption:

1. **State the assumption clearly** — one sentence, specific to this market
2. **Why the market treats it as true** — what evidence or inertia keeps this assumption in place? Reference specific competitors or market behaviors from the research
3. **What breaks it** — what would have to be true for this assumption to be wrong? Be specific about the mechanism, not just "technology changes"
4. **Early signals** — are there any signs in the research data that this assumption is already weakening? Cite specific evidence. If there are no signals, say so explicitly

These should be structural, not surface-level. "Customers want lower prices" is not an assumption — it's a truism. "The market assumes property managers will always need on-premise software because of data sensitivity requirements" is a structural assumption.

---

## Stage 3: Investor Destruction Test

**Prompt:**

You are a world-class investor evaluating a new entrant into the {{industry}} market. You've read the Unspoken Insight (Stage 1) and Foundational Assumptions (Stage 2). You have access to all the research data.

Write **5 sharp questions** designed to expose fatal flaws in any business plan entering this market. These should be the questions that make founders uncomfortable — the ones that probe real structural risks, not softballs about "how will you acquire customers."

For each question:
1. **The question** — specific, pointed, grounded in evidence from the research
2. **Your answer using only research evidence** — what does the data actually tell us? No speculation, no "it's likely that..." — only what the evidence supports
3. **Evidence quality rating** — rate the strength of your answer: STRONG (multiple corroborating sources), MODERATE (some evidence but gaps), or WEAK (insufficient evidence to answer confidently)
4. **Gap identification** — if the evidence is MODERATE or WEAK, state exactly what additional research would be needed to answer this properly

---

## Stage 4: Stress Test Loop

**Prompt:**

Review your Stage 3 answers. For each answer you rated MODERATE or WEAK, or any answer where you feel the argument is thin:

1. **State the weakness** — why is this answer unconvincing?
2. **Construct the strongest possible version** — using all available evidence, what's the best argument you can make? Steel-man it
3. **Identify where it still breaks** — even the strongest version has limits. Where?

Rate each original Stage 3 answer on a 1-10 scale:
- 9-10: Evidence is compelling and multi-sourced
- 7-8: Reasonable answer with some gaps
- 5-6: Answer exists but is speculative
- 1-4: Can't meaningfully answer with available evidence

For any answer scoring 7 or below, run the stress test above.

**Max iterations: 2.** After two passes, consolidate remaining gaps into a clear "Open Questions" list rather than continuing to loop.
